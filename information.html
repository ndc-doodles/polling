Introduction

Tamil Nadu, located at the southern tip of India, is famous for its ancient Dravidian culture, classical arts like Bharatanatyam and Carnatic music, and rich heritage of temples. With Chennai as the capital, it is a hub for education, technology, and industries.

  • Population: ~75 million
  • Official Language: Tamil
  • Major Cities: Chennai, Coimbatore, Madurai, Tiruchirappalli
  • Famous Temples: Brihadeeswarar Temple, Meenakshi Temple, Rameswaram

Etymology

The name "Tamil Nadu" translates to "Land of the Tamils." It emphasizes the ethnic and linguistic identity of the Tamil people, one of the oldest living civilizations with a rich literary and cultural tradition.

The word “Tamil” represents the language, literature, and culture, while “Nadu” means country or region. Tamil Nadu as a state was officially formed in 1956 after the States Reorganization Act.

Geography

Tamil Nadu features diverse geographical regions including coastal plains along the Bay of Bengal, fertile river valleys like the Cauvery delta, and hill ranges such as the Western and Eastern Ghats.

  • Rivers: Cauvery, Vaigai, Thamirabarani
  • Hills: Nilgiris, Western Ghats, Eastern Ghats
  • Coastal area: 1,076 km along Bay of Bengal

Climate

Tamil Nadu has a tropical climate, hot and humid along the coasts, and cooler in hill stations. The state receives most rainfall during the northeast monsoon (October–December) and occasional southwest monsoon showers.

History

Tamil Nadu has a history spanning over 2,000 years. Ancient Tamil kingdoms like Cholas, Pandyas, and Cheras contributed immensely to art, architecture, and literature.

Politics & Major Parties

Tamil Nadu’s politics is dominated by Dravidian parties since 1967. Major parties include DMK and AIADMK.
